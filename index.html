Rethink with AI
Hand a thought to AI and see it differently. Paste anything — a worry, an opinion, a decision — or arrive straight from My Thoughts, then pick a lens: Reframe it (spot unhelpful thinking patterns, find a kinder balanced view), Challenge it (a fair devil's advocate — the strongest counter-case, hidden assumptions, blind spots), Other perspectives (a mentor, a skeptic, a pragmatist, future-you), or Sharpen & act (distill what you mean and get concrete next steps). Switch lenses with no re-typing, or run all four. The AI is framed as a thinking partner, not a therapist — it never diagnoses, invents no facts about you, and every reflection is badged as a prompt to think with, not advice. Static HTML + a Groq-backed Vercel function; your thought is sent only when you ask.

🪞#26
MirrorScope
Interviews are a mirror of reaction. Paste an interview transcript and decode it per speaker as a sectioned visual report: an at-a-glance scorecard, a turn-taking river and dual-line sentiment timeline, a word cloud, a two-sided word-shift chart, a pronoun ternary and stance map, a reaction map of how they answer each topic, a Q→A scatter, and an annotated transcript with stance words coloured — plus reaction-to-triggers (type a word, see how they responded when the other person raised it). It also measures mirroring — Language Style Matching (Ireland & Pennebaker 2010): how closely the two speakers fall into the same function-word style, with a convergence sparkline — profiles the questioner (open vs closed questions, follow-up and leading-phrasing rates), and reads readability & richness (Flesch, vocabulary, sentence rhythm). Every chart is hand-rolled and computed in the browser (offline, never sent anywhere); an optional AI layer adds a qualitative Big-Five radar + personality read, always badged inference, not a validated score. A passcode-keyed decode library (cross-device, or on-device offline) saves decodes under a person's name — search/filter them, merge variant names, run a combined deep read that pools all of one person's interviews into a single profile, and compare two people side by side. Compare two interviews to see the stance drift between them. Single-file HTML + four Vercel functions.

Syed's Research Book
A browsable, time-flowed view of a 24-month systematic-review corpus — 9,388 unique papers across 167 management / organizational-behavior constructs, every paper DOI-keyed and Scopus-graded. Search and filter the whole library, walk the construct taxonomy, and read a co-occurrence map + construct × construct matrix now powered by a fresh tier of 408 clean constructs extracted from 79,000 paper titles + abstracts — it reads each paper's real construct mix (not a single fetch-tag), counts how often any two or three constructs are studied together across 40,000+ multi-construct papers, surfaces the strongest pairs, under-studied links and research-gap white-spaces, then jumps to those papers. Plus a "when each construct surged" trends heatmap and a folded-in Term Trends treemap — 50 years of OpenAlex research vocabulary, animated. Every record is Syed's own hand-verified coding — transformed, never invented; the corpus layer is machine-matched and verify-badged. The literature spine the rest of the suite reads from.
